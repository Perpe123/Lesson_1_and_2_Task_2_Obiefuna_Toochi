What do you know about diabetes?

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disease characterised by elevated levels of blood glucose which leads over time to damage to the heart, blood vessels, kidney, eye and nerves.

Diabetes can be type 1 diabetes in which the pancreas produces little or no insulin or type 2 in which the body's ability to utilize available insulin is impaired leading to inability to process sugar.

Gestational diabetes

This is a condition characterized by an elevated level of glucose in the blood during pregnancy, typically resolving after the birth of the child.

Routine blood glucose monitoring and monitoring of the baby are highly recommended. Regular physical exercise, weight reduction and consumption of healthy diet are also beneficial in such condition.

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential and can only be obtained from the diet. This Omega-3 dietary deficiency is compounded by the fact that pregnant women become depleted in Omega-3s because the fetus uses Omega-3s for its nervous system deployment. It has a number of benefits which includes:
  • Taking DHA during pregnancy may reduce the risk of pre-term birth.
  • Omega-3 fatty acids, especially DHA, are building blocks of the baby's brain and retina.
  • DHA supplementation during pregnancy may positively impact a child's risk of Childhood obesity.
  • Better infant response to stress.
  • Maternal mental well-being.
  • DHA intake is essential during breastfeeding and formula feeding.
